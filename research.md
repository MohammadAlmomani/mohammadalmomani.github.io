# Research Areas

## 1. Short-Term Voltage Stability and Voltage Trajectory Analytics

This research direction develops data-driven and measurement-based indices for detecting, quantifying, and classifying short-term voltage instability from voltage trajectories. The work focuses on delayed voltage recovery, oscillatory voltage behavior, Lyapunov exponent-based features, entropy-based metrics, EMD-based signal decomposition, and real-time stability margin assessment.

**Representative papers:**

- Trajectory-Based Nonlinear Indices for Real-Time Monitoring and Quantification of Short-Term Voltage Stability
- Novel Data-Driven Indices for Early Detection and Quantification of Short-Term Voltage Instability from Voltage Trajectories
- Enhanced Entropy-Based Metric for Characterization of Delayed Voltage Recovery

**Keywords:** short-term voltage stability, voltage trajectory, delayed voltage recovery, FIDVR, Lyapunov exponent, entropy metric, EMD, KL divergence, real-time stability monitoring.

## 2. DER and Smart Inverter Reactive Power Dispatch

This direction focuses on optimization-based control of smart inverters and DER-rich distribution systems. The work includes sensitivity-aware reactive power dispatch, IEEE 1547-style inverter control modes, Volt-VAR, Volt-Watt, Watt-VAR, PQ/PV operation, TSO–DSO coordination, and real-time dispatch implementation.

**Representative papers:**

- TSO-DSO Coordinated Reactive Power Dispatch for Smart Inverters with Multiple Control Modes Real-Time Implementation
- Extended Sensitivity-Aware Reactive Power Dispatch Algorithm for Smart Inverters with Multiple Control Modes

**Keywords:** smart inverter, DER, TSO-DSO coordination, reactive power dispatch, Volt-VAR, Volt-Watt, Watt-VAR, RLS, MILP, SOS1, OpenDSS, IEEE 13-bus, IEEE 123-bus.

## 3. Low-Frequency Oscillation Detection and Damping

This direction investigates low-frequency oscillation identification, damping, and control in power systems. It includes ringdown analysis, wind-generation impacts, ANN-based damping controllers, pre-disturbance PMU data, damping ratio estimation, and electromechanical mode identification.

**Representative papers:**

- Ringdown Analysis for Low-Frequency Oscillation Identification
- Low Frequency Oscillation Analysis for Dynamic Performance of Power Systems
- The Impact of Wind Generation on Low Frequency Oscillation in Power Systems
- Damping Undammed Low Frequency Oscillations in Power Systems: An ANN-Based Approach Using Pre-Disturbance Data

**Keywords:** low-frequency oscillation, ringdown analysis, electromechanical modes, damping ratio, ANN controller, PMU data, wind generation, power system stability.

## 4. Low-Inertia and Converter-Dominated Power Systems

This research direction studies the dynamic behavior of future power systems with high renewable penetration and reduced synchronous inertia. The work includes virtual inertia emulation, inertia estimation, synchronverter dynamics, SPWM/SVPWM comparison, and converter-based stability support.

**Representative papers:**

- Future Low Inertia Power Systems: A Comprehensive Review of Virtual Inertia Emulation Techniques and Inertia Estimation Methods
- The Dynamic Response of SPWM vs. SVPWM Synchronverter

**Keywords:** low inertia, virtual inertia, inertia estimation, synchronverter, grid-forming inverter, converter-dominated grid, renewable energy integration, frequency response.

## 5. PMU Placement, WAMS, and Transient Stability Prediction

This direction focuses on measurement infrastructure and AI-based stability prediction. It includes optimal PMU placement, connectivity matrix algorithms, modified connectivity matrix algorithms, global-binary PMU placement, WAMS, and ANN-based transient stability prediction.

**Representative papers:**

- Connectivity Matrix Algorithm: A New Optimal Phasor Measurement Unit Placement Algorithm
- Modified Connectivity Matrix Algorithm
- Global-Binary Algorithm: New Optimal Phasor Measurement Unit Placement Algorithm
- Prediction of Transient Stability Using Wide Area Measurements Based on ANN

**Keywords:** PMU placement, phasor measurement unit, WAMS, power system observability, connectivity matrix algorithm, modified connectivity matrix algorithm, global-binary algorithm, transient stability, ANN.

## 6. Renewable Energy Modeling, Islanding Detection, and Protection

This direction covers AI-based modeling of renewable generation, islanding detection, and protection of compensated transmission lines. It includes photovoltaic array modeling using genetic algorithm and cuckoo optimization, ANN-based islanding detection, and numerical/distance relay studies.

**Representative papers:**

- Application of Artificial Intelligence Techniques for Modeling and Simulation of Photovoltaic Arrays
- Islanding Detection Method Based Artificial Neural Network
- Performance Analysis of the Distance Relay Characteristics in a Compensated Transmission Line
- Modelling and Testing of a Numerical Pilot Distance Relay for Compensated Transmission Lines

**Keywords:** photovoltaic array modeling, genetic algorithm, cuckoo optimization algorithm, islanding detection, ANN, distance relay, compensated transmission line, FACTS, protection.
